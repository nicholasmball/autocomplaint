UX Design: Mailto/Clipboard Delivery Gaps

Building on the Requirements step — designing the 4 identified gaps in the existing StepDeliver flow.

1. Interaction Flow Overview

The existing 7-step wizard already handles most of the delivery flow. This design focuses on the 4 gaps identified in the Requirements step. The flow below highlights where changes occur:

Step 1–4: Input → Step 5: Review & Generate → Step 6: Deliver (Gaps 1, 4) → Step 7: Success (Gap 3) → History (Gap 3)

New Component When the URL-encoded complaint body exceeds 1500 characters, a warning banner appears above the delivery buttons.

4. Gap 4: Recipient Email Validation in Wizard

Enhancement Ensure recipient email is always available by Step 6.

Current State

Step 1 collects recipient type and name. Recipient email is only entered at Step 6 (StepDeliver). This works but means users must type the email at the last moment.

Proposed Change

Add an optional email field to Step 1 (StepRecipient) that pre-populates Step 6. Keep the email field editable on Step 6 as a fallback.

7. Mobile Considerations

  • Warning banner: Full-width, text wraps naturally. Icon stays top-aligned.
  • Delivery buttons: Stack vertically on <480px (existing grid-cols-1 breakpoint).
  • "Mark as sent" prompt: Buttons stack vertically on small screens.
  • Mailto link: Opens native mail app on mobile — more reliable than desktop. Warning is still shown for consistency.
